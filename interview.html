Client interview

I got to interview Dr. Ishaq, and below are the question numbered and his answers to them. He founded the International Child Art Foundation (ICAF) in 1997 in Washington, DC. to serve American children as their national arts organization and the world's children as their global arts organization. Over the past twenty-five years, nearly five million children worldwide have participated in the art programs he has developed, most notably the Arts Olympiad.

1) What is the job you do?
He is the chairman of ICAF and he runs the organization.
He runs the school art program all over the world and also runs the national art festival at the mall in Washinton D.C. every year. While doing all of that he also helps publish and design the school art magazine every four years.

2) What would you say are some of your strengths and weaknesses?
One thing he said was that was part of both is that he has to have creativy for this job to help design and run everything but then also it is pretty hard for one main person to run most of everything on his own.
When he is looking to maybe hire someone he looks for them to be create and think outside the box for the company. To also get things done.

3) What are some things that are most rewarding from this job?
Dr. Ishaq touched hard on what they do and how it impacts the children. He does what he does to be a good human being and to show that God wants human beings to create a better future for our upcoming children and family in the world.

4) What kinds of promblems do you deal with?
The work load can be hard for him. He has longer days at work, not like a job where you go in at seven and go home at six. He is usually staying later and because he is higher up he also will take the work home with him. He said, "in a way the job will take over your life".

5) What impact does this have on the world?
Kids making art is making more of a visual and art community. He talked about as we look around now we don't see very much of what kids do or create, only most of what adults do and what they make. He wants to really show the world what and how kids can create and how it can impact the world by making it more joyful.

6) What impact does your job have on the people?
With the ICAF being a company for about 25 years there are many people that have been and still are being impaced. There are kids he remembers helping years ago and he might keep in touch with some or research them and find out later that they have grown up and are doing great things with thier life. So it is great seeing that their lives were impaced and are showing it in their story with what they are doing now in life years later.

7) What are a couple skills to have for this job?
A couple things you need to have for this job are, good human relationships. Because you will be working with people and especially kdis it is really good to be able to communicate and have a strong desire to do that. Another thing that is good to have is inspiration. inspiration for the kids and inspiration for others to maybe just have a better outlook on life.
