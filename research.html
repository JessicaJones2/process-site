The Benefits of Art for Kids.
- It encourages fine motor skills, neural development, and problem-solving abilities and that it can be used effectively to teach and understand other key subjects such as reading, writing, math, and science.
- Therapists tell us that art is valuable because it allows children to process their world, to deal with sometimes scary emotions in a safe way, and because it gives them critical sensory input.
- Artist tells us that art is important for its own sake as a source of beauty and expression as well as imply for the process of creating.
- Kids tell us that art is fun, an activity they enjoy.
- Parents tell us that rat is vital to their families because it keeps everyone engaged and happy and helps with the sometimes-difficult transitions of the day.

Why Art?
- Helping to develop mentally, socially, and emotionally.
- Creating art may boost young children’s ability to analyze and problem-solve in many ways.
- Their fine motor skills approve.
- By counting pieces and colors they learn the basics of math
- When children experiment with materials, they dabble in science.
- When kids feel good while they are creating, art helps boost self-confidence.

How to talk to children about their art.
Question to ask are… - “tell me about your picture” as a way to encourage conversation.
- “what do you like about your work?”
- “I see you used a lot of colors. Why did you use blue?” describe what you see.
- “I noticed you made the lines go up and down.” Talk about their action.
- “you made a lot of pictures today, which one do you want to hang up?” encourage and support.
- “what part did you enjoy the most?” ask about the process.
